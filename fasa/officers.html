OFFICER BOARD

Marijo Manois

  • Why did you choose to take on the task of becoming an officer for this upcoming centennial year?
    • I choose to become President during such a monumental year because I am determined to weave purpose and meaning into everything this organization executes its programming and events. Furthermore, being President doesn't mean I determine every single action FASA sa UW takes, rather it is my duty to cultivate an environment where officers and members utilize their cultural background as platform for leadership and success. Through this term I, alongside my officer board will make sure that our history is told and that the legacy of Filipino of Organizations inspires positive change within our community.
  • What part of FASA caused you to keep coming back?
    • Through FASA I was able to discover the history of Filipino & Filipino American leadership on this campus and throughout the world. It is through interacting with the community and being inspired by their stories and resilience that fuel my own ambitions to make a positive impact.
  • I Am More Than ___.
    • I am more than a university student. I am leader, a woman of color and my success will be for those who sacrificed so much to get me here.
  • Recommended classes:
    • CHID 250 : Critical Community Organizing, The Tagalog Courses, Any AES class with Bonus and if you're able any African American, Indigenous, Latinx History classes.
  • Fun Fact:
    • Ask me about my freestyle rapping skills.
  • Social Media Handles:
    • Feel free to add me on Facebook haha I don't really do social media

Alex Gonzaga

  • Why did you choose to take on the task of becoming an officer for this upcoming centennial year?
    • I chose to take on this opportunity for a few reasons. First, I missed the officer life and how much fun it was to serve the community and our members. Second, I wanted to step up to be able to use this year as a way of honoring our ancestors and surrounding community at UW and the impact they had in the development of UW and the shaping of what the Filipino identity is today. Lastly, I chose to take on the task because I wanted to give back to the community that welcomed me in, gave me a second home, and made me realize how important it is to know your heritage. As a returning officer, I look forward to what we can accomplish on such a momentous year ahead.
  • What part of FASA caused you to keep coming back?
    • The people. FASA brings in people of all backgrounds, those who identify as Filipino, and those who identify as Filipino American. Each individual has something new to offer when they step into the space that FASA holds, and I love learning about each individuals' story that makes them who they are. Without a strong membership, I don't know where FASA would be today, or if I would have even been so inspired by our members to serve as an officer for a second time.

    • My culture's delicious food.

Saralyn Santos

  • Why did you choose to take on the task of becoming an officer for this upcoming centennial year?
    • I've been in Sayaw for the last two years, and loved every minute of it! I was really able to break out of my shell, make a lot of new friends, and learned so much about the Philippines that I'd never known before. I went from someone who could barely do the Electric Slide to someone who could sprint through a 45-minute set at Northwest Folklife; I have Sayaw to thank for that.
  • What part of FASA caused you to keep coming back?
    • I really appreciated the way everyone in FASA just welcomed me in and accepted me as one of the family. Even though I was really shy and awkward my freshman year, and didn't talk all that much, people remembered my name and said hi to me whenever they saw me. To young freshman me, something as little as that meant a lot. As I got deeper into FASA, I loved the opportunities I had to learn more about our culture and our history. FASA has given me a family and a deeper appreciation of what it means to be Filipino, and I'm eternally grateful for that. If I had to sum it all up, I'd say Filipinx friends and free food (the most important F's of my college experience) kept me coming back.
